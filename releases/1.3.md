# EarthCal 1.3.8

## Earthen Auspicer Release

**Release date:** 2026-04-13

In time for EarthDay, we are excited to make a major new release. We've done a full review of the codebase to resolve bugs and streamline overall functionality. However, the biggest change is in an upgrade to Earthcal's core functionality itself.  Heck, its an upgrade to the functionality of the very concept of a modern calendar.  To capture the essence of this functionality, we're making use of an ancient word **auspices**.

In ancient Rome, a seer known as an augur would look for signs in the world around them—birds, the Moon, the stars—to discern whether a day was favorable, or auspicious, for a particular kind of action. Over time, this kind of discernment became clouded by superstition. Yet beneath it was a serious intuition: that the world is patterned, and that time is not neutral. Today, we are rediscovering how deeply meaningful many of these indicators can be, as ecological rhythms, biological cycles, and celestial movements reveal measurable influences on life on Earth.

EarthCal already performs astronomical calculations for a given day across lunar, solar, and planetary cycles, displaying them visually and numerically. The next question is: **what do these patterns mean?** To answer that, we have cross-referenced insights from indigenous traditions, chronobiology, and biodynamics to build a date interpretation system.

Now, when you add a date item, EarthCal can show the **Earthen auspices** for that date. Whether the Moon is waning and ascending, at apogee, or full and descending, EarthCal now interprets these astronomical conditions through a semantic engine grounded in astronomical data, biodynamic theory, and ancestral wisdom.

This is not astrology, and it is not AI. It is a structured interpretive system based on observed celestial cycles and a curated knowledge framework.

Expand the data windows in the app to explore the new interpretive layer.
