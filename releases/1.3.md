# EarthCal 1.3.8 #

**Subtitle**
## Earthen Auspicer Release ##

**Release date:** 2026-04-13

In time for EarthDay, we are excited to make a major new release of EarthCal.

This release includes a full review of the codebase to resolve bugs and streamline installation and overall functionality. But the biggest change is not under the hood. EarthCal now connects with the **Earthen Auspicer API**.

As you know, EarthCal already performs astronomical calculations for a given day across lunar, solar, and planetary cycles, displaying them visually and numerically. The next question is: **what do these patterns mean?** To answer that, we have cross-referenced insights from indigenous traditions, chronobiology, and biodynamics to build a date interpretation system.

Now, when you add a date item, EarthCal can show the **Earthen auspices** for that date. Whether the Moon is waning and ascending, at apogee, or full and descending, EarthCal now interprets these astronomical conditions through a semantic engine grounded in astronomical data, biodynamic theory, and ancestral wisdom.

This is not astrology, and it is not AI. It is a structured interpretive system based on observed celestial cycles and a curated knowledge framework.

Expand the data windows in the app to explore the new interpretive layer.
